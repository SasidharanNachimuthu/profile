---
title: The Folder of Unsent Letters
date: 2025-04-21
---

On my laptop there is a folder called `_unsent`. It holds about sixty
essays, some five years old. None of them have been published. A few are
finished; most are not. They exist for one reason: to keep the published
work honest.

## What lives there

- Drafts that turned out to be arguments with myself.
- Letters to people I never sent because the act of writing was enough.
- Three abandoned book proposals.
- A pamphlet on the ethics of analytics dashboards, which I still might
  finish.
- The first version of nearly every essay on this site.

I do not delete them. The folder is not a graveyard; it is a kiln. The
pieces that come out of it are different from the ones that went in, and
better for the time spent inside.

## Why keep them

There is a particular kind of clarity that only comes from writing
something you have decided in advance not to publish. It removes the
performance from the sentence. No one is watching. The argument can be
wrong, the metaphor can be embarrassing, the voice can wobble. None of it
will be held against you, because none of it is going anywhere.

> The freedom to write badly in private is the prerequisite for writing well
> in public.

Most of the things I am proud of on this site began life in `_unsent`. They
sat there for a season, or three. I returned to them when I had something
new to add, or — more often — when I realised I had nothing more to add and
the piece had been finished for months without me noticing.

## A small invitation

If you write, even occasionally, I would gently suggest making your own
version of this folder. Call it whatever you like. Promise yourself that
nothing in it will ever be seen. Then write the thing you are most afraid
to write, and let it stay there.

You will be surprised by what comes back changed.
